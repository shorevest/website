Strategy

Asset-backed private credit in China

ShoreVest evaluates secured opportunities through entry basis, claim priority, enforceability, and exit route.

Return outcomes are underwritten around documentation, collateral control, and resolution path rather than a directional call on China’s economy.

Margin of safety
Low loan-to-value / cost-to-value entry
Sub-strategies
Asset-Based Lending · Asset Restructuring · Debt Resolutions
Standard
Claim priority · enforceability · defined recovery

The opportunity

China remains structurally under-allocated

China is the world’s second-largest credit market and one of the most systematically under-allocated in global portfolios.

That gap reflects the difficulty of operating here: regulatory complexity, enforcement uncertainty, and a creditor landscape that rewards local presence over capital alone.

Why this persists

Demand

Credit vacuum

The contraction of shadow banking and a large stock of recognized NPLs have pulled credit out of the market, widening spreads for disciplined secured lenders.

Barriers

High barriers to entry

Relationships, licensing, and local credibility take years to build. Competition in the situations ShoreVest targets remains structurally thin.

Execution

Execution complexity

Outcomes turn on control, documentation, and local process. That capability cannot be acquired quickly.

Three sub-strategies

One underwriting standard

Deployment weights shift with the opportunity set. Underwriting does not.

Across asset-based lending, asset restructuring, and debt resolutions, each position must clear the same threshold before capital is committed.

Common principles applied to every deal

Conservative basis Entry priced below assessed collateral value
Defined horizon Shorter-dated positions with clear exit paths
Cash yielding Emphasis on near-term recovery

Sourcing & diligence

Origination discipline

Sourcing is relationship-led and not intermediated. Opportunities are advanced only where legal control and recoverability can be evidenced through diligence.

  1. Sourcing

    Bank, AMC, legal, and servicing channels across the firm’s onshore networks.

  2. Screening

    Collateral quality, claim priority, and enforcement path reviewed before committee.

  3. Diligence

    Full legal and asset-level underwriting with stressed downside testing.

  4. Committee

    Only structures with defined recovery routes are approved for deployment.

Recovery

Multiple paths to monetization

Enforcement is a capability, not the strategy. Most positions resolve through negotiated settlement or secondary sale.

When court processes are involved, secured-creditor outcomes depend on perfected collateral, a clean documentation chain, and the right venue.

The availability of multiple resolution paths means recovery rarely depends on any single route.

Downside protection

Risk controls

Downside controls are embedded in both deal-level underwriting and portfolio construction.

Underwriting controls

  • Conservative entry basis with stressed recovery assumptions.
  • First-lien claims and enforceability reviewed under PRC-law documentation.
  • Limited reliance on refinancing to realize value.

Portfolio controls

  • Diversification by borrower, collateral, province, and vintage.
  • Continuous surveillance from origination through disposition.
  • Predefined exit pathways to reduce duration and execution risk.

Downside protection

Collateral first

Every position is underwritten around legal control and recoverability, so value does not depend on spread, refinancing, or market direction.
