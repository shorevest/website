Investment process

Underwriting and risk controls

Downside analysis is applied at both the transaction and portfolio level.

Transaction-level considerations

  • Pricing and structure are evaluated against downside scenarios, expected recovery value and required margin of safety.
  • Priority of claim, documentation quality and enforceability are reviewed with counsel under the applicable legal framework.
  • Collateral position, perfection, control mechanics and realization pathways are assessed where relevant to the investment.
  • Repayment, refinancing, restructuring, asset sale and enforcement alternatives are mapped before capital is committed.

Portfolio-level considerations

  • Portfolio construction incorporates limits and ongoing review of concentration, duration, liquidity and execution risk.
  • Positions are actively monitored through repayment, refinancing, restructuring, sale, enforcement or other resolution.
  • Exposures are reviewed across borrowers, counterparties, collateral types, geography, sector and investment vintage where relevant.

Recovery risk

Risk qualification

Recoveries can be affected by legal process, counterparty behavior, market liquidity, asset values and the timing and cost of enforcement.

ShoreVest emphasizes legal control, collateral coverage where applicable and practical recoverability, rather than depending primarily on borrower growth or favorable market direction.
