# Work at ShoreVest

ShoreVest is a private credit firm focused on complex debt situations in China.

Since 2004, we have invested across asset-backed lending, asset restructuring, and debt resolution. Our work requires more than conventional credit analysis. We assess the business, the assets, the capital structure, the legal rights, the stakeholders, and the practical routes to repayment or recovery.

We recruit selectively and seek professionals of the highest caliber—people with exceptional intellectual ability, sound judgment, and the discipline to perform consistently at a demanding standard.

## A Different Kind of Credit Work

Conventional credit analysis often focuses on earnings, leverage, liquidity, and repayment capacity. These considerations remain important, but they are not sufficient for the situations ShoreVest evaluates.

We also consider:

- The assets supporting the investment
- The quality and enforceability of creditor rights
- The strength of the transaction structure
- The incentives of borrowers, sponsors, and other stakeholders
- The potential for value leakage or deterioration
- The options available if the original repayment case fails
- The likely path to restructuring, enforcement, or recovery

This requires financial analysis, legal understanding, commercial judgment, and local execution experience.

The objective is not simply to determine whether a borrower is likely to repay. It is to understand the full range of outcomes, identify where value may be protected or lost, and assess whether the investment remains sound under adverse conditions.

Few professionals develop this breadth of judgment. We seek those with the ability and ambition to do so.

## Working at ShoreVest

ShoreVest maintains a focused team across its offices in China.

Professionals work closely with senior colleagues and contribute throughout the investment process, from initial assessment and structuring through execution, monitoring, restructuring, and realization.

Team members are expected to understand the complete investment case rather than operate within narrowly defined responsibilities. They must identify the issues that are material to the decision, explain their conclusions clearly, and take responsibility for the quality of their work.

Our focused structure places a premium on individual quality. We appoint only professionals of the highest caliber, and every hire must strengthen the firm.

We value independent thought, careful analysis, direct communication, and consistent execution. Responsibility increases as judgment and reliability are demonstrated.

## Investment Careers

Our investment professionals assess businesses, assets, and credit structures across private credit and special situations.

Depending on the role, responsibilities may include:

- Identifying and evaluating investment opportunities
- Conducting company, industry, and market research
- Analyzing financial performance, cash flow, liquidity, and leverage
- Evaluating assets, collateral, and downside protection
- Reviewing ownership structures, creditor rights, and transaction documents
- Developing repayment, downside, and recovery cases
- Supporting transaction structuring, negotiation, and execution
- Monitoring investments and identifying changes in risk
- Assessing restructuring, enforcement, and recovery alternatives
- Presenting investment recommendations to senior decision-makers

We look for professionals who can combine detailed analysis with a clear investment view.

Technical ability is essential. The greater challenge is determining which facts are material, how they affect the range of outcomes, and whether the available protections can be relied upon in practice.

## Professional Development

Professional development at ShoreVest is based primarily on substantive work, close collaboration, and direct feedback.

Team members gain experience across different stages of the investment process and assume greater responsibility as their judgment develops.

They learn to:

- Investigate an investment beyond its headline financial metrics
- Identify weaknesses before they result in loss
- Assess whether structural and legal protections are effective in practice
- Distinguish material risks from secondary issues
- Adapt an investment view as circumstances change
- Make clear recommendations when information is incomplete

The objective is not simply to develop technically capable analysts. It is to develop professionals who can be trusted with complex situations and consequential decisions.

## Our Standards

### Analytical Depth

We examine the business, assets, documentation, and stakeholder dynamics until the principal sources of risk and value are understood.

### Independent Judgment

Professionals are expected to form their own conclusions and support them with evidence.

### Precision

In credit, an incorrect assumption, overlooked provision, or misunderstood asset can change the outcome. Details must be examined carefully.

### Commercial Relevance

Analysis must support a decision. Volume and complexity are not substitutes for clarity.

### Accountability

People are responsible for the accuracy, quality, and completion of their work.

### Professional Candor

Weak assumptions and unsupported conclusions should be challenged directly and constructively.

### Integrity

We act responsibly in our dealings with investors, counterparties, and colleagues, including when the more convenient course would be different.

## Recruitment Fraud Notice

Candidates should be cautious of emails, messages, or social media communications falsely claiming to represent ShoreVest.

Official recruitment communications will take place through recognized ShoreVest channels. ShoreVest will never ask a candidate to make a payment as part of the recruitment process.

Candidates should not provide financial information, passwords, or sensitive identity documents through unverified channels. Suspicious communications should be reported using the contact information published on ShoreVest’s official website.
